The Transsyberian Railways

Route
The Transsyberian Railways go from Moscow throughout the country to the east, right away to Vladivostok on the Eastern shore. Before reaching the lake Baykal it forks down to the South towards Irkutsk, and then after going around the lake it continues to the Southeast through Ulanbaatar (the capital of Mongolia) to reach Beijing as it's end-station. It's an immense trip through the whole of Asia, not crossing such a great variety of cultures, but these few you can meet on the way are stunning enough themselves to make you pack your things and force you onto your butt for a while.

On the train
We had it quite comfortable in a 4 persons couchette just the two of us with Miha. The ticket to Irkutsk costs around 100 EUR, plus we bought some food for the trip of course, though we heard there are people selling stuff at every bigger station on the way. The wagon was quite old but in a pretty nice condition, everything worked and every wagon had a personnel of 3 people to look after things - they kept the place fairly clean too. During the four days we read some thick books, slept a lot, and when we got fed up with doing nothing we bought some vodka and got drunk. ÷)

The villages we were passing were made of wooden houses, the only brick buildings were practically the train stations - most of them were built in 1952-53 as the labels show. The landscape was rather boring too, a huge steppe and nothing much to see. We got known with some Russian fellows and had a couple of funny conversations with an english-russian/russian-english travel dictionary... so I went over to drink with them somewhat and we turned up laughing at each other's attempts to say something - I didn't know a thing in Russian and they didn't know a thing in any other language. ÷) When I crawled back to my place I put a nylon bag on the door next to my head just in case...
